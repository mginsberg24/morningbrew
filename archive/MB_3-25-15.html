MARKET MINUTE: TOO GOOD TO BE TRUE

The indices forgot to pack a lunch, losing strength midway through Tuesday’s market hours. By approximately 12 P.M. EST, information regarding a rise in the Consumer Price Index (CPI), aka inflation, as well as housing prices both contributed to a reversal of the indices, handing back all initial gains accumulated in the A.M. Earnings season is a day closer, and remains an underlying factor for this week’s volatility.

U.S. MACRO: CPI, HOME SALES SHOW PROMISING SIGNS

The Implications

Contrary to the Fed’s pessimistic outlook, promising new data shows growth in consumer spending and demand for new homes, as Americans remember how much they love buying things. This new data has placated concerns about housing and spending, although investors remain hesitant to break out in song, as signs of a more stable economy may encourage the Fed to raise interest rates in the near future.

Delving into the Data

CPI rose for the first time since October, indicating renewed consumer confidence and spending. CPI monitors the prices of a plethora of goods, ranging from transportation services to food. Meanwhile, the annual rate of new home sales shot up 7.8% to roughly 539,000 sales per year for the month of February, its highest level since February 2008. Despite this positive data, there remain doubts, with annual growth rates for the U.S. economy projected to drop as Americans lounge poolside this summer.

WORLD MACRO: SAUDI'S GREEDY OIL PUSH 

Three months after OPEC’s Thanksgiving weekend meeting went horribly awry, controversy regarding OPEC kingpin Saudi Arabia is present and worse than before. The question: just who do they think they are? Normally producing around 10 million barrels per day (bpd), the Saudis have shown a deliberate disregard of the already excess supply, contributing an extra 350,000 bpd. This power move puts OPEC well over the quota given to them a mere month ago. As Saudi Arabia continues to suck oil from the sand and add to the supply, an overflowing oil market will create price instability.

CORPORATE PRIMER: GOOGLE GOES EXECUTIVE SHOPPING
 

Morgan Stanley's Ruth Porat Joins Google

Don’t believe it? Google it: As of yesterday, Morgan Stanley finance chief Ruth Porat is officially joining the Google team as CFO. Porat served as co-head of the Technology Investment Banking Group and lead banker on a number of tech IPO’s, and has been consistently praised for her creativity. These tech/finance qualifications will allow her to address growing shareholder concerns over missed earnings reports and huge spending on speculative projects, all while retaining the creativity and vision that Google is known for.

Trend of Talent Migrating West

It may not just be Porat’s Silicon Valley upbringing that finally pushed her from Wall Street to the tech world. Recently, Goldman Sachs banker Anthony Noto joined Twitter as Chief Financial Officer, Credit Suisse banker Imran Kahn joined Snapchat as Chief Strategy Officer, and Goldman Sachs managing director Sarah Friar joined Square Inc. as Chief Financial Officer. The list goes on. Wall Street may not be hurting for talent, but it has serious competition for smart, driven professionals.

GERMANWINGS 9525 PLANE CRASH

The Tragedy

At 5:53 AM ET, Flight 9525 went down in a remote part of the French Alps, taking the lives of 150 passengers and crewmembers. A black box has been recovered from the plane, which was traveling from Barcelona to Dusseldorf, and an investigation is underway. This is an absolutely horrific accident and we send our prayers to the family members of the victims.
Between Germanwings 9525, Delta 1086 and AirAsia 8501, plane crashes are at the forefront of media consumers’ minds. While these accidents are horrendous when they do occur, we would like to address those afraid of flying, now more than ever.

Plane Crashes by the Numbers

  • You're less likely to die in a plane crash than you are from a bicycle accident, being hit by lightning, or even being stung by a bee.
  • There were around 32 million airline departures in 2013. Less than one flight in 300,000 of those flights had an accident, and only one in 3,000,000 was fatal.
  • Turbulence is generally a "convenience issue, not a safety issue."

BUILDING BLOCKS: CHINESE OPPORTUNITY IN EUROPEAN REAL ESTATE

The Tables Have Turned: As the Eurozone continues to struggle, cunning Chinese investors are looking to take advantage of low real estate prices. For years, European investors flocked to the prosperous Chinese markets in search of major growth plays. Now, the very men whom the Europeans made rich prosper off of their misery. Chinese investors are capitalizing on the weak Euro and poor economic conditions by increasing controlling interest across European assets such as real estate.

TODAY IN TECH: NFL TAKES TO THE WEB

Bold Strategy, Cotton: Keeping with the times, the NFL will be airing one mid-season football game on the Internet and not cable television. The week 7 game between the Bills and the Jaguars, a game that will not be sorely missed, will be a test to gauge demand in the market for exclusive online viewing. The game will be auctioned off to the highest bidding digital platform. Some believe that this is the beginning of a new age of sports broadcasting, while skeptics think it will have little impact on the way we enjoy America’s favorite sport.

STARTUP OF THE DAY: MEERKAT

Ben Rubin’s Bay Area-based start-up, Meerkat, is on fire. The latest and greatest broadcasting social network made its debut at South by Southwest (SXSW), an interactive music festival in Austin, Texas. So, what’s the secret sauce? Meerkat enables users to live stream their lives via twitter feed. Whether the user is in a mosh pit at a concert or behind home plate at a baseball game, the applications for this technology are endless. As for growth, the mobile-based mongoose is doing just fine. Meerkat signed up 120,000 users in its first two weeks, and has grown 30-40% a day since SXSW on March 13th.

Liquidity -- The ease with which an asset can be bought or sold in the market. Liquidity is defined by higher trading activity and is considered to be safer than illiquid assets since the investor can more easily convert to cash.
