MARKET MINUTE: THE MARKETS ROAR

Despite ferocious volatility, the indices and U.S dollar are both within reaching distance of historic highs. All of the major indices closed higher on Friday, with the Dow Jones Industrial Average leading the way, up over 150 points. Oil continues to lag historically low prices, yet it rallied 3% with Friday’s gains, finishing the week at $45.72/ barrel. After initially mixed reactions, investors have finally reached positive consensus regarding the Fed’s Wednesday announcement of a delayed interest rate hike.

THE U.S MARKETS: NASDAQ HITS 15 YEAR HIGH

The Record

The Nasdaq reached a 15-year high on Friday, gaining 0.7% and ending the day at 5048.62. The Nasdaq is now within striking distance of its record high in March of 2000 during the dot-com bubble. The Nasdaq has been outperforming other major indices thus far in 2015, and one of its biggest gainers, Apple, has recently been added to the Dow Jones.

The Future

The Nasdaq continues to fly uninhibitedly on the wings of encouraging growth and data. Index leaders have stabilized over time, and newcomers to the index have showed productive growth. Most investors say the only thing this index has to fear is fear itself (investor sentiment), and the policy news from the Fed has encouraged the equity market universally.

THE MACRO LANDSCAPE: GREEK AUSTERITY 

The Latest
 

Greek Prime Minister Alexis Tsipras has still yet to solidify an economic bailout from Eurozone finance ministers. At a meeting in Brussels last week, German Chancellor Angela Merkel made it clear that Germany is willing to provide the necessary financial support, under the condition that the birthplace of western civilization lays out plans for paying off debt and reaching targets. Mr. Tsipras will have a difficult time getting his far-left Syriza party to accept the types of reform that Eurozone creditors are looking for.

Here’s What to Look for

 
Mr. Tsipras and Ms. Merkel plan to meet in Berlin today. Although a majority of European citizens would welcome a Greek exit from the euro, most political figures in the region view this outcome as undesirable and will work towards a compromise with Greece. However, if Mr. Tsipras continues to stubbornly try to convince Merkel to extend financial support with looser budgetary restrictions, talks could go like last month’s meeting, in which no agreement was reached.

THE MACRO LANDSCAPE: THE YUAN

Stronger Yuan = Stronger Economy?
 

The strength of Chinese-backed currency may indicate the stability and future prosperity of the economy. As the Yuan continues to depreciate against the greenback, investors had no other choice than to bet against the Chinese economy or take their capital elsewhere. Yet, the recent return of the Yuan (up 0.9% against the dollar last week) has brought back the two-sided monetary fluctuations that economies are used to. China’s economic forecast remains cloudy, yet a few gamblers and underdogs believe that Beijing will continue to defend the Yuan’s strength through economically stimulative fiscal policy. One ray of light: US exports are cheaper, for now!

VENMO CONTINUES TO GROW

The Business Side
 

Founded in 2009, California-based Venmo has experienced a skyrocket in transactions as Generation Y has begun to “chuck their checkbooks” for alternative options. With $1.3M in funding, Venmo is leading the way in mobile payments, with competitors such as Square chasing its lead.

VENMO BY THE NUMBERS

  • $700M - Value of mobile transactions processed in the 3rd quarter of 2014
  • $90B - The estimated U.S mobile transaction market in 2017
  • 800,000- The average number of transactions each week for Venmo in 2014
The Social Media Effect of Venmo
 

Venmo has become a social media platform through its payment memo line, where you tell your transaction partner what you are paying for or requesting. QZ.com analyzed Venmo transactions for a week, and found that emojis are commonly used in this memo line. About 25% of all transactions utilized emojis, and the most frequent emojis (pizza, wine and beer), suggest Venmo is mostly used for “eating, drinking, and partying.” Making appearances in nearly 10,000 payments per weekend, pizza appears to own the weekend as far as Venmo-ers are concerned.

General business discussion: Real Estate to Venture Capital

BUILDING BLOCKS: SPG UPS OFFER FOR MACERICH 

Simon Property Group increased its offer to $16.8B in an ongoing effort to buy out rival Macerich. The mall developer, who tried to ask nicely at first, now has no choice but to pursue a hostile takeover of Macerich. Macerich responded negatively to initial advances, and switched up its board structure to make it tougher to be bought out. SPG’s insatiable desire to buy out Macerich and the tense situation it created is indicative of a larger trend, where poor returns at low-end malls have corporations scrambling for the more lucrative high-end properties.
 

TODAY IN TECH: BOOM IN CYBER SECURITY IPOs

The Industry

With recent, high-profile data breaches at major U.S. corporations including Sony, JPMorgan Chase, and Anthem Inc, fearful businesses are driving significant growth in the cybersecurity industry. With global security spending poised to grow 8.2% in 2015 to $77B, several cybersecurity firms are planning to capture value by IPOing this year. One of these firms, Boston-based Rapid7, provides software and services that help businesses understand security risks. Rapid7 represents more than 3,500 clients, including Amazon and AmEx, and is seeking a public valuation in excess of $1B.

The Risks

To the untrained eye, life is great in the cyber security industry. Massive industry growth, crazy valuations. Stock prices of companies like FireEye, Qualys Inc and Palo Alto Networks are up 38%, 24%, and 19% this year, respectively. Looks  perfect! Too perfect. Indeed, investing in this market is not without risk. These companies, akin to 3-D printing, are plagued by volatility. FireEye, for example, dropped more than 70% in less than three months in 2014, when its CEO and other insiders sold company shares. Investors beware: several firms share identical price trajectories to that of FireEye inc.

STARTUP OF THE DAY: MINIBAR


Drunk driving: Minibar Delivery promises delivery of alcohol orders over $25 in under an hour. The app launched in New York last May and is now available in 16 cities. Since its inaugural bottle was popped in New York, Minibar has raised a total of $1.8 million in seed funding. Minibar does not charge a delivery fee, instead making money through transactions with its partnering stores. Although Minibar Delivery may be the largest player in the space by revenue, there is no shortage of competition. Similar companies like Drizly, Saucy, Drinkfly, Klink Delivery, Thirstie, and Swill show that as long as more cutesy business names exist, growth will continue for alcohol delivery services.
